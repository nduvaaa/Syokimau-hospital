When to Visit the Emergency Department

Recognizing medical emergencies that require immediate attention

Emergency Cases

  • Chest pain or difficulty breathing
  • Severe bleeding or deep wounds
  • Sudden numbness or weakness
  • Severe head or eye injuries
  • Broken bones or dislocated joints
  • High fever with headache and stiff neck
  • Poisoning or drug overdose
  • Severe burns or electrical injuries
  • Seizures or loss of consciousness

Urgent Care Cases

For non-life-threatening conditions, our Urgent Care Center might be appropriate:

  • Minor cuts requiring stitches
  • Sprains and minor fractures
  • Fever without severe symptoms
  • Mild to moderate asthma
  • Rashes and minor allergic reactions
  • Earaches and eye infections
  • Urinary tract infections
  • Mild dehydration

If you're unsure whether your condition requires emergency or urgent care, call our nurse advice line at (800) 555-9876.

Emergency Care FAQs

Common questions about our emergency services

What should I bring to the Emergency Department?

If possible, bring a list of your current medications, allergies, medical history, and your insurance information. However, in true emergencies, don't delay seeking care â€“ we can gather this information later.

How does the triage system work?

When you arrive at our Emergency Department, a triage nurse will assess your condition based on the severity of your symptoms. Patients with life-threatening conditions are seen first, regardless of arrival time. We strive to provide the most urgent care to those who need it most immediately.

What if I need to be admitted to the hospital?

If your condition requires admission, our emergency team will coordinate with the appropriate department for your continued care. We'll handle all the necessary arrangements for your transfer to an inpatient unit.

Does insurance cover emergency visits?

Most insurance plans cover emergency services. However, coverage varies by plan. We provide care regardless of your ability to pay, especially in emergency situations. Our financial counselors can assist you with questions about coverage and payment options.

What follow-up care is provided after an emergency visit?

Upon discharge, you'll receive detailed instructions for your recovery and any necessary follow-up care. We can help schedule appointments with specialists if needed and provide prescriptions for medications. Our care coordinator may contact you to ensure you're recovering well.

In Case of Emergency

For life-threatening emergencies, call 911 immediately. For all other emergencies, our team is ready to provide prompt and effective care 24/7.
